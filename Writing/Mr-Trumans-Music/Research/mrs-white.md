# Mrs. White

Grace White was not your average piano teacher. She was trained in Vienna by, Theodore Leschetizky, a prominent teacher of the time. Leschetizky taught scores of prominent musicians, most notably Ignacy Jan Paderewski, a popular piano virtuoso in the 1890s and early 1900s. 

#   


  


The twice weekly voyages to his piano lessons would clearly be trans-formative events for young Harry Truman.

  


  


Harry received instruction from Mrs. White on a Steinway concert grand piano in the spacious front parlor of the White residence at 27th and Brooklyn in what was then the eastern edge of Kansas City. It must have been a special treat for young Harry to play on a Steinway Grand. 

  


The magnificent voice of a concert grand is a stark contrast to the Kimbal upright which was in the Truman home in Independence.

  


Grace White born December 12, 1860 in Jackson Mississippi; died January 29, 1937 in Kansas City, Missouri

  


Edwin C. White born November 25, 1830 Richmond, VA; died August 7, 1907
