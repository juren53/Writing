# Source Intake Log — Mr. Truman's Music

## How to Use This Log

When new source material arrives:
1. Drop the file in the correct `Sources/` subfolder
2. Add a row to the **Running Intake Log** below
3. Update the relevant chapter entry in `Book/outline.md`
4. Create a `Research/` note if the item is substantive enough to extract from

**Naming convention for new files:**
```
[TYPE]_[SUBJECT]_[CONTEXT]_[DATE-if-known].[ext]
Examples:
  PHOTO_Truman-at-piano-WH-1952.jpg
  DOC_Truman-letter-to-Pollock-1953-04.pdf
  AUDIO_Truman-on-music-interview.mp3
  For HSTL items, keep catalog number + append description:
  72-3113_Truman-at-piano-JFK-watching.jpg
```

## Notable Finds from Photo Review

- **72-3113**: If confirmed as JFK, this is an extraordinary image — a sitting president watching his predecessor play piano. No other photo in the collection carries the same historical weight.
- **95-23**: Truman at a clavichord with a Mozart-era portrait on the wall is an unusually composed and meaningful image — deliberate pairing of Truman with classical tradition.
- **65-2173**: Truman reviewing sheet music with a Black man in the White House is visually striking and potentially connects his musical life to his civil rights legacy.
- **2009-1504/1505**: If the woman is Lauren Bacall, these document the famous NPC Canteen moment — Bacall on or beside the piano, February 1945. A defining pop-culture image of Truman.
