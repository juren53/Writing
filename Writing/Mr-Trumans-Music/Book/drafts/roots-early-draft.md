# Mr. Truman's Music - Roots

Musical Roots

  


A young boy hurries down the sidewalk with a bundle tucked under one arm. His closest friend, Charlie, catches a glimpse of Harry as he hurries away from the neighborhood. Being his best, Charlie isn't about to deride his friend for disappearing twice a week after school while the rest of the neighborhood gang is out playing. This boy is obviously is a 'young man on a mission' and he wasn't afraid of being different and doing things differently from his neighborhood chums.

  


As most boys in their early years - - they have no idea what life has in store for them. So it was with young Harry. Little did he know that in fifty years he will he would be the leader of the most powerful nation on the planet, immersed in a global global conflict fighting for its very existence. 

  


But at this moment, at the age of 10, young Harry is focused on his first passion - - music. And today he must catch the electric trolley to Kansas City. There his piano teacher, Mrs. E. C. White teaches piano out of her home at 27th and Brooklyn. It was a large two story brick house and a magnificent Steinway was in spacious front parlor. Twice a week young Harry would make the trip to have Mrs. White drill him on the fundamentals of proper piano. And Harry took to it like a fish to water. So much that Harry was considered her best student.

  


The trolley ride from Independence to Kansas City did more than just transport him a couple dozen miles. It took him into a completely different world. The sights and sounds of that trolley ride were trans-formative for young Harry. And in Mrs Whites home Harry was again transported into a world of music and culture that must have seemed thousands of miles from his neighborhood in Independence. Not that Independence was in anyway backward, in fact just opposite. It was know as town of culture and thinkers with with its share of world travelers. But Independence had Southern roots that differentiated it from Kansas City that was could be refered to as a "Yankee town".

Mrs. E. C. White was not your average piano teacher by any means.
